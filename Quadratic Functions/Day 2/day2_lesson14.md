# Lesson 14: Choosing the Best Method

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Decide intelligently between methods.

## Concepts list
- method selection
- efficiency
- exactness
- strategy

## Teach me
1. The best method depends on the structure of the quadratic. If it factors quickly, factorise. If the vertex matters, complete the square. If nothing is obvious, use the formula.
2. Mathematical maturity means choosing, not just applying. The same equation can often be solved in more than one valid way.
3. A method is good if it is accurate, efficient, and reveals useful structure. Those three goals are not always identical.
4. In exam conditions, method choice saves time and reduces error.
5. Ask yourself first: what does the question want me to know, not just what does it want me to calculate?

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## Study moves
- Spend 10 minutes reviewing the examples before touching exercises.
- Do 3-5 easier questions first, then 2-3 harder ones.
- Write one sentence after each question: “What idea did this question really test?”
- Keep an error log with two columns: mistake and fix.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Is the fastest method always the best method? Give a mathematical example to support your answer.
2. For one quadratic, solve by two different methods and compare what each method reveals.
3. Why might a teacher insist on a slower method even when a calculator gives roots immediately?
