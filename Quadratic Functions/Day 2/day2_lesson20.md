# Lesson 20: IB Style Mixed Mastery

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Blend representation, reasoning, and problem solving under exam conditions.

## Concepts list
- synthesis
- reasoning
- exam communication
- multi-step problems
- reflection

## Teach me
1. IB-style questions often hide the main idea inside a context or a chain of linked parts. Read the whole structure before calculating.
2. Show enough working to make your reasoning visible. Even when you know the answer, the explanation is part of the mathematics.
3. When stuck, anchor yourself in one of the core lenses: roots, vertex, discriminant, or graph shape.
4. A mixed problem is usually not testing ten separate ideas. It is testing whether you can choose the right one at the right time.
5. Finish the two-day course by solving slowly, checking aggressively, and reflecting on patterns in your mistakes.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## Study moves
- Spend 10 minutes reviewing the examples before touching exercises.
- Do 3-5 easier questions first, then 2-3 harder ones.
- Write one sentence after each question: “What idea did this question really test?”
- Keep an error log with two columns: mistake and fix.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. What is your decision process when you meet a new quadratic question and do not immediately know the method?
2. Why is it dangerous to rely on recognising surface patterns only, instead of understanding structure?
3. Choose one topic from the two-day plan and explain how it connects to at least two other topics in the plan.

## Exit check
- Can you explain this lesson without using the textbook wording?
- Can you identify the key feature of a quadratic in less than 20 seconds?
- Can you tell which method or representation is most efficient here?
