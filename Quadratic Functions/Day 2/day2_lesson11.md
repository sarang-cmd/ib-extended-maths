# Lesson 11: Solving by Factorisation

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Solve quadratic equations efficiently when factorisation is available.

## Concepts list
- null factor law
- equation solving
- checking solutions
- illegal cancellation

## Teach me
1. Factorisation is the fastest solving method when the quadratic splits cleanly. Once you have a product equal to zero, use the null factor law.
2. If (x-3)(x+5)=0, then x=3 or x=-5. The power comes from turning one difficult equation into two easy linear ones.
3. Never cancel a variable factor unless you know it cannot be zero. That mistake destroys valid solutions.
4. After solving, check at least one solution mentally or by substitution. It is quick and catches sign mistakes.
5. This method is algebraically clean and graphically meaningful because the solutions are x-intercepts.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Why is factorisation both a solving method and a graph interpretation method?
2. A student solves x^2=5x by dividing by x and gets one answer. Explain precisely what went wrong.
3. Can factorisation show when a quadratic has a repeated root? Explain.
