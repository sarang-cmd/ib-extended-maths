# Lesson 18: Optimisation

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Use the vertex to solve maximum and minimum problems.

## Concepts list
- optimisation
- maximum
- minimum
- feasible values
- interpretation

## Teach me
1. Optimisation means finding the greatest or smallest possible value of a quantity. For quadratics, the vertex is the key.
2. If the parabola opens upward, the vertex gives a minimum. If it opens downward, the vertex gives a maximum.
3. In modelling, not every algebraic solution is realistic. Dimensions cannot be negative, and counts usually must be whole numbers.
4. The strongest optimisation answers interpret both the x-value and the corresponding function value.
5. This topic is one of the clearest examples of why graph features matter in real contexts.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Why is the turning point the correct place to look for an optimum in a quadratic model?
2. A model gives a maximum profit at x=12.4 products. Why might the final real-world answer not be exactly 12.4?
3. Explain the difference between the maximum value of a function and the x-value where the maximum occurs.
