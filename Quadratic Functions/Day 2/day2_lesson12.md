# Lesson 12: Solving by Completing the Square

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Solve exactly when factorisation is awkward.

## Concepts list
- exact solutions
- radicals
- perfect square form
- no real roots

## Teach me
1. Completing the square works even when simple factorisation fails. That is why it is one of the foundational exact methods.
2. The goal is to turn the equation into something like (x-p)^2=q, because then solving becomes a square-root step.
3. If q is negative, there are no real solutions. That is not a failure of the method; it is valid mathematical information.
4. This method is slower than factorisation but more revealing. It shows the centre of symmetry of the quadratic while solving it.
5. A strong student uses this method to understand, even if they later choose the quadratic formula for speed.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Why must the coefficient of x^2 be 1 before the standard completing-the-square step?
2. Explain how solving by completing the square is related to finding the vertex of the graph.
3. A quadratic has no real roots. How can completing the square reveal that faster than graphing?
