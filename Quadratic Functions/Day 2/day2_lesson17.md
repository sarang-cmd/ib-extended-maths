# Lesson 17: Inequalities and Sign Thinking

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Reason about where quadratics are positive or negative.

## Concepts list
- sign diagrams
- intervals
- greater than
- less than
- graph regions

## Teach me
1. Solving a quadratic inequality means finding where the graph is above or below the x-axis, not just where it touches it.
2. Roots split the number line into intervals. On each interval, the sign of the quadratic stays consistent unless you cross a root with odd multiplicity.
3. A quick sketch often helps more than symbol pushing.
4. This topic forces you to connect algebraic factors, graph shape, and interval reasoning.
5. Be careful with words like positive, non-negative, greater than, and at least. Those change endpoint inclusion.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Why are the solutions to a quadratic inequality usually intervals rather than isolated numbers?
2. How does the sign of a affect whether values between the roots are positive or negative?
3. A quadratic never cuts the x-axis. Can it still satisfy y>0 for all x? Explain.
