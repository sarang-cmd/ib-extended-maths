# Lesson 16: Intersections and Tangency

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Understand where quadratics meet lines or other functions.

## Concepts list
- simultaneous equations
- points of intersection
- tangent
- double root

## Teach me
1. When a line and a quadratic meet, solve the equations simultaneously. Substitution usually turns the problem into a quadratic equation.
2. If there are two intersection points, the line cuts the parabola. If there is one repeated intersection, the line is tangent.
3. Tangency means the resulting quadratic has discriminant zero. That is a very efficient test.
4. You can also compare two quadratics by setting them equal. The x-values you get tell you where the graphs cross.
5. This topic is a bridge between graphing and algebraic solving.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Explain why a tangent line leads to a repeated root in the intersection equation.
2. Two graphs intersect once. Does that always mean the line is tangent? Explain by considering other function types.
3. Why is setting two equations equal logically valid when looking for intersections?
