# Lesson 13: Solving with the Quadratic Formula

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Use the universal solving method confidently.

## Concepts list
- quadratic formula
- substitution of a b c
- exact form
- solution types

## Teach me
1. The quadratic formula works for every quadratic equation once it is written in standard form ax^2+bx+c=0.
2. Its strength is reliability. Its weakness is that students often substitute signs incorrectly, especially when b or c is negative.
3. Before using the formula, rewrite the equation carefully and identify a, b, and c from the final standard form only.
4. After solving, simplify radicals where possible and judge whether decimal approximations are useful for context.
5. Think of the formula as a universal tool, not the first tool for every question.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Why is the quadratic formula more general than factorisation?
2. A student gets a correct decimal answer but wrong exact form. Why can that still matter mathematically?
3. Explain how the discriminant is built into the quadratic formula rather than being a separate idea.
