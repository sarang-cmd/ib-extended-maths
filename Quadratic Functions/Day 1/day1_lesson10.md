# Lesson 10: Day 1 Synthesis

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Unify forms, graph features, and interpretation.

## Concepts list
- multiple representations
- equation-graph links
- self-checking
- concept map

## Teach me
1. By now, you should be able to move between words, equations, and sketches. That flexibility is the real goal.
2. If you know roots, think factor form. If you know a turning point, think vertex form. If you know a y-intercept and need expansion, think standard form.
3. Whenever you solve, ask what the answer means on the graph. Whenever you graph, ask what equation information is being displayed.
4. This lesson is about coherence: the topic becomes easier once you stop treating each method as separate.
5. Your revision target is not just getting answers. It is making every answer explain at least one feature of the quadratic.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## Study moves
- Spend 10 minutes reviewing the examples before touching exercises.
- Do 3-5 easier questions first, then 2-3 harder ones.
- Write one sentence after each question: “What idea did this question really test?”
- Keep an error log with two columns: mistake and fix.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Describe one quadratic in three different forms and explain what each form reveals best.
2. Why is it possible for two students to use different methods and still produce the same correct quadratic?
3. Give a real-life scenario where the vertex matters more than the roots, and justify your choice.

## Exit check
- Can you explain this lesson without using the textbook wording?
- Can you identify the key feature of a quadratic in less than 20 seconds?
- Can you tell which method or representation is most efficient here?
