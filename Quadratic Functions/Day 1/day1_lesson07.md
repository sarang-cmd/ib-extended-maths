# Lesson 7: From Standard Form to Vertex Form

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Translate between forms fluently.

## Concepts list
- standard form
- vertex form
- translation
- equivalent expressions
- parameter meaning

## Teach me
1. Standard form y=ax^2+bx+c is compact, but vertex form y=a(x-h)^2+k is more descriptive. It tells you exactly where the graph turns.
2. In vertex form, h gives the horizontal shift and k gives the vertical shift. The sign inside the bracket often confuses students: x-h means the shift is to the right by h.
3. The value of a still controls opening and width. So the shape is inherited from y=ax^2 and then translated.
4. Being able to move between forms is a high-level skill because it lets you choose the best representation for the task.
5. This is one of the most common conceptual themes in IB-style questions: same function, different forms, different insights.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Which form is best if you know the turning point? Which is best if you know the roots? Justify both choices.
2. A quadratic is given in vertex form and another in standard form. Explain how you would verify they are the same without graphing.
3. Why does changing k in y=a(x-h)^2+k move the graph vertically but not change its width?
