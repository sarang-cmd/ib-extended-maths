# Lesson 4: Intercepts and Roots

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Connect algebraic solutions with graphical intercepts.

## Concepts list
- x-intercept
- y-intercept
- root
- zero
- function value

## Teach me
1. The y-intercept is the easiest point to find: set x=0. In standard form y=ax^2+bx+c, the y-intercept is c.
2. The x-intercepts happen where y=0. That means finding x-intercepts is the same as solving the quadratic equation.
3. A root, a zero, and an x-intercept all describe closely related ideas. The first two are algebra language; the third is graph language.
4. If the graph touches the x-axis, you may have one repeated root. If it crosses the x-axis twice, you have two distinct real roots.
5. Always ask whether you are solving an equation or describing a graph. The mathematics is linked, but the wording matters.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Can a quadratic have a y-intercept but no x-intercepts? Give an example and justify graphically and algebraically.
2. Two quadratics have the same y-intercept and same axis of symmetry. Must they be identical? Explain.
3. Explain the difference between “the function equals zero” and “the graph has y-value zero”.
