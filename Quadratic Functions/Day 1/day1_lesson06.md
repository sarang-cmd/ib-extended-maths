# Lesson 6: Completing the Square Visually

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Understand why completing the square reveals the vertex.

## Concepts list
- perfect square
- completing the square
- rewriting form
- translation

## Teach me
1. Completing the square rewrites a quadratic so its geometric structure becomes visible. Instead of a messy standard form, you get a shifted square expression.
2. When you rewrite y=x^2+4x+1 as y=(x+2)^2-3, the vertex becomes obvious: left 2 and down 3 from the basic graph y=x^2.
3. If the coefficient of x^2 is not 1, factor it out first before completing the square. This is the part many students skip, and then everything falls apart.
4. The real value of this method is understanding, not speed. It shows why the vertex formula works rather than just handing you the answer.
5. This method also prepares you for solving quadratic equations exactly.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Why is completing the square more revealing than factorising when you want the vertex?
2. A student adds the wrong constant when completing the square but still gets a parabola. Explain exactly what kind of information becomes unreliable.
3. Without solving fully, explain what feature of a graph becomes immediate once a quadratic is written in vertex form.
