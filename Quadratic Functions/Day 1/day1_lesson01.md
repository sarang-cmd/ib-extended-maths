# Lesson 1: What a Quadratic Function Is

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Recognise a quadratic, identify the coefficient of x^2, and connect algebra to the parabola.

## Concepts list
- quadratic function
- standard form y=ax^2+bx+c
- parabola
- leading coefficient
- real-world meaning

## Teach me
1. A quadratic function is a rule where the highest power of x is 2. That single fact is what makes the graph curve instead of staying straight.
2. In y=ax^2+bx+c, the value of a can never be zero. If a becomes zero, the x^2 term disappears and the function is no longer quadratic.
3. The graph of a quadratic is called a parabola. You can think of it as the path made by a projectile, the arch of a ball, or a smooth turning curve.
4. The coefficient a controls the overall opening: positive means the graph opens upward, negative means it opens downward.
5. At this stage, do not try to memorise everything at once. The key is to connect equation, table, and graph as three views of the same object.

## What to be able to do
- Explain the idea in your own words.
- Move between equation and graph.
- Notice which form of a quadratic is most useful.
- Check whether your answer is mathematically and contextually sensible.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. A student says “all curves are quadratic”. Give one example that looks curved but is not quadratic, and explain how you know.
2. Without graphing, compare y=2x^2+3x-1 and y=-2x^2+3x-1. What features stay related, and what features must change?
3. Explain why y=ax^2+bx+c with a=0 cannot have a turning point in the same way a quadratic does.
