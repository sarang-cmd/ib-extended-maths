# Lesson 8: Discriminant and Graph Behaviour

> Note: This guide gives exercise **references only** from the H&H books, not full copied questions.
> Use the book for the original exercise text.

## Focus
Use the discriminant to predict roots and x-axis interaction.

## Concepts list
- discriminant
- number of roots
- touching vs cutting
- repeated root

## Teach me
1. The discriminant is b^2-4ac. It tells you what kind of real roots a quadratic equation has before you solve it fully.
2. If the discriminant is positive, the graph cuts the x-axis twice. If it is zero, the graph touches the x-axis once. If it is negative, the graph misses the x-axis completely.
3. This is one of the cleanest links between algebra and graphing in the whole topic.
4. A repeated root means the x-axis touches the parabola exactly at the vertex. That is why the discriminant is zero there.
5. Do not treat the discriminant as a random test. It is really a shortcut for understanding the geometry of the graph.

## IB-style conceptual questions
1. Explain why a negative discriminant means there are no real x-intercepts, but the graph still exists.
2. Can two different quadratics have the same discriminant but different graphs? Explain with examples or reasoning.
3. A quadratic has discriminant zero. What can you deduce about the vertex without solving the full equation?
